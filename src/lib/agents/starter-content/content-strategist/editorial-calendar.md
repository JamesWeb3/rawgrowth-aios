# Editorial Calendar

The calendar is the spine. Without it you ship reactive content, the audience smells it, the algorithm punishes you. Plan 4 weeks ahead. Always.

## Pillar topics

Pick 4-5 pillars. Every piece of content maps to one. If a topic doesn't fit a pillar, it doesn't ship.

Example pillar set for a B2B founder:

1. **Build in public** (product updates, behind-the-scenes, lessons)
2. **Operator playbooks** (specific tactics with numbers)
3. **Industry takes** (POV on what's happening in our space)
4. **Customer stories** (case studies, testimonials, results)
5. **Founder essays** (longer-form, opinionated, evergreen)

Ratio per week (rough): 30% playbook, 25% build-in-public, 20% takes, 15% stories, 10% essays.

## Weekly cadence

Mon: playbook post (LinkedIn long-form)
Tue: build-in-public update (Twitter thread + LinkedIn cross-post)
Wed: short video (Reels + Shorts + TikTok, same cut)
Thu: take / commentary on industry news (Twitter, LinkedIn)
Fri: long-form blog or essay (Substack + LinkedIn newsletter)
Sat: rest
Sun: customer story or repurpose roundup

If a slot is empty 24 hours out, we publish a repurpose, not nothing.

## Repurposing rules

One pillar piece feeds the whole week. Treat long-form as the source of truth, then atomize:

```
1 long-form blog (1500 words)
  -> 5 LinkedIn posts (one per main argument)
  -> 3 tweets (the spiciest takeaways)
  -> 1 short video (90 seconds, the hook + payoff)
  -> 1 carousel (the framework as visual)
  -> 1 newsletter section (the synthesis)
```

Same idea, six formats. Different audience hits each one.

## Calendar template

Spreadsheet, refreshed every Friday for the next 4 weeks:

```
Week of | Slot     | Pillar           | Title / hook                    | Format    | Owner   | Status     | Link
--------|----------|------------------|---------------------------------|-----------|---------|------------|------
W22 Mon | Playbook | Operator         | "How we run weekly forecast"    | LI long   | Pedro   | Drafted    | <doc>
W22 Tue | BIP      | Build in public  | "Shipped agent hire v2"         | TW thread | Pedro   | Scheduled  | <link>
W22 Wed | Short    | Operator         | "3-step pipeline forecast"      | Reel      | Editor  | In edit    | <doc>
```

Status values: idea -> drafted -> reviewed -> scheduled -> published.

## Measurement

Track per piece:

- Saves (the truest signal of value)
- Replies / comments (the conversation signal)
- Shares (the reach signal)
- Profile visits (the funnel signal)
- DMs (the conversion signal)

Top performers go into the "winners" doc and get a sequel within 30 days.

Underperformers (bottom quartile) get autopsied: was it the hook, the format, the time, or the topic? One reason per autopsy. Document it.

## Rules I don't break

- No publishing without a hook tested against the founder's voice
- No reposting without a fresh angle in the caption
- Saves matter more than likes. Optimize for saves.
- If a pillar doesn't perform after 6 weeks, we kill it
